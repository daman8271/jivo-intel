● Internal data-product brief · June 2026

JIVO's e-com control tower for edible oils.

The JIVO e-com app is the company's single internal command centre for its online edible-oils business — canola, mustard, sunflower, groundnut, soyabean, rice-bran, olive/pomace, sesame and ghee, plus non-oils (seeds, spices, juices, honey). In one workspace it tracks the whole value chain — from Jivo Wellness (the parent factory that makes and bills the oil) through Jivo Mart (JM), out to 8–10 platforms (Amazon, Swiggy, Blinkit, Zepto…), and all the way to consumer sell-through — measured everywhere in litres and split Premium vs Commodity. Across 13 pages built on 41 raw tables it answers, daily: what's the category mix, what's moving, are we filling orders, where's the stock, and is marketing spend paying off. It was built fast (~1 month) and is live — powerful, but with a few hollow corners this brief calls out honestly.

The business model & value chain

One litre of oil, re-sold down a four-rung chain. Volume shrinks as stock is held back at each stage; price rises as each stage adds its margin. These are the four Home KPI cards (Jun 2026).

Volume shrinks, then bounces back

811k → 672k → 485k L: each stage holds back stock as inventory (≈139k built at Wellness, ≈188k at JM). But Secondary (560k) > Primary (485k) — consumers pulled more than Jivo shipped this month, i.e. platforms are destocking. A persistent Secondary>Primary gap is the early warning for next-month stock-outs — the single most important cross-page signal.

The ladder is reproducible in the data

Inside master_po the per-litre columns step up the same way: without-margin ₹202/L → exclusive (with distributor margin) ₹213/L → inclusive (with GST) ₹225/L. The price ladder is the reliable monotonic signal; the litre counts are not. Rung-1 (₹180) is owner-asserted — it sits above what the extracted data can recompute.

Premium vs Commodity vs Other — split inside every card

June 2026 Primary oils: PREMIUM 233,401.7 L vs COMMODITY 225,302.0 L → premium-mix ≈ 50.9%. The Primary card (484,975 L) also carries an ≈26,271 L OTHER (non-oil) head that the category charts exclude.

Anomalies & caveats

The app was built fast (≈1 month) and is live — powerful but with hollow corners. These are the things to know before trusting any number blind.

Hollow feature

JM↔platform reconciliation is empty

inventory-match returns {"match":null} for all 10 platforms — the screen meant to reconcile JM stock vs platform stock does not actually function yet.

Hollow feature

JM Primary has no source object

Rung-2 (672,739 L) has no leaf/table; prim_master_po is empty and the JM-as-vendor proxy fails (JIVO MART delivered 0 L Apr–Jun). It sits upstream of the SSOT.

Never populated

category-sku-breakdown is dormant

Schema supports Category→Sub-cat→Platform→SKU for all 10 platforms, but every snapshot (2023→2026) is a single "Uncategorized" row with 0 SKUs.

Mislabelled source

top-skus carries source=primary

top-skus, category-breakdown and category-litres default to source:"primary" — they render sell-IN, not the sell-out you'd expect near the Secondary surface.

Stale feed

jiomartSec frozen to 2026-04-15

JioMart secondary is ~2.5 months behind the live platforms (06-25); it has no secondary__jiomart page and is excluded from YoY. JioMart inventory is near-dead too (sellable=0).

Empty platforms

CityMall & Zomato are shells

Both are wired but *Sec and *_inventory tables are 0 rows (expected_empty). Their secondary sell-out is effectively un-tracked.

Off-model

Amazon runs a parallel PO pipeline

primary__amazon.source = reporting."Amazon PO", absent from master_po/total_po/total_po_zbs. It's ⅓ of ordered litres (429k of 964k) yet has the worst fill-rate (42.1%) and can't be reconciled against the same table.

Owner-asserted

Wellness Billing isn't in the SSOT

811,616 L @ ₹180/L (and all 4 card ₹/L) are owner-screenshot values; master_po starts below the Wellness→Jivo invoice, so the data can't recompute them.

Broken roll-up

soh-doh totals are zeroed

Top-level totals read 0 for swiggy/blinkit/zepto/bigbasket (Amazon's are fine) and the four sub-keys repeat byte-identical — use region-doh or raw tables for those.

No native view

Distributor has no dashboard

There is no distributor__* doc; the page is built ad-hoc over master_po's vendor columns, and the only roll-up is platform-grained (fulfilment-health), not vendor-grained.

Under-fed

amazon_price_data is thin & dirty

The price-match engine exists structurally but rests on a 2-date snapshot (192 rows); jm_price is null on 72% of rows with implausible outliers (A2 Ghee 1L ₹129 vs ASP ₹1,579).

Definition trap

"Value" ≠ consumer GMV

Raw *Sec tables store gross MRP; dashboards' "Done Value" = SecMaster.sales_amt ≈ 0.40× that (realised, after margin). Summing raw GMV overstates revenue ~2.3×.

Non-additive

Amazon range views double-count

amazon_sec_range_master_view holds overlapping date ranges — a naive SUM(shipped_litres) gives 2.08M L vs the true 195,294 L. The dashboards de-overlap; raw sums must not.

Gated, not broken

Non-Amazon marketing is server-gated

The ad/price/coupon dashboards render live only for Amazon; the underlying tables exist for all platforms but the per-platform dashboards are restricted server-side (a 400/404 is the API's choice).

Lifecycle noise

delivered_qty=0 ≠ missed

The EXPIRED + CANCELLED tail (6,294 + 3,061 lines, ~21%) inflates "ordered". BigBasket shows 64% zero-delivered yet 82% fill — the lagged window exists precisely to strip this noise.

Upload lag

Everything trails real-world by ~a week

fulfilment-health bakes a 7-day lag into its window — a hint that uploads (the only way data enters) trail real POs by roughly a week. Treat "missing" recent data as upload-lag, not a model gap.

Self-assessment

How confident are we in this model?

This brief is built from four domain deep-dives, each independently verified against the extracted SSOT (41 tables + the app's own dashboard docs), not just restated from context. Confidence is high where numbers reconcile and lower where the data simply doesn't contain the object.

HIGH
The data-backed core: Primary/Secondary/Inventory/Distributor mechanics, the 41 tables, the ₹/L ladder, fill-rate, SOH/DOH, premium-mix — all cross-checked and tied out.
MEDIUM
Marketing beyond Amazon (gated), JioMart (stale/dormant), and the realised-value (~0.40× MRP) formula that's computed server-side.
LOWER
JM Primary & Wellness Billing (owner-screenshot rungs, not in the SSOT) and write-only Uploaders (no captured payloads).

What would need owner confirmation

  • The true source of JM Primary (672,739 L) — no table, vendor proxy fails. Is it an upstream stock-transfer ledger we never extracted?
  • Provenance of Wellness Billing 811,616 L @ ₹180/L — confirm it's an authoritative figure, since the SSOT can't recompute it.
  • Are category-sku-breakdown and inventory-match intended-but-unbuilt, or broken? Both are hollow today.
  • The SecMaster.sales_amt realisation rule (~0.40× MRP) — the one money figure not reproducible from raw.

What we're sure of

  • The value chain is one flow of oil re-sold down four rungs; the price ladder is the reliable signal, litre counts are not monotonic.
  • master_po is the join hub — Primary, Pendency, POS, both target tracks & fulfilment all read it (Amazon excepted).
  • premium-mix is the universal axis, classified per-SKU, appearing on Home, Insights, Targets & Secondary.
  • The pipeline is Uploaders → Data → domain pages → Insights → Home → Reports.
